A tibial plateau fracture is a break in the upper part of the shinbone (tibia) that involves the knee joint. Here's a simple breakdown:
Tibia: The shinbone, the larger of the two bones in your lower leg.
Plateau: The flat top part of the tibia that connects to your knee.
Fracture: A break or crack in the bone.
So, a tibial plateau fracture happens when the top part of your shinbone (which forms part of your knee joint) gets cracked or broken. This type of injury often results from high-impact trauma, like a car accident or a fall from a height, and can cause significant pain, swelling, and difficulty in moving the knee.
Treatment might involve rest, wearing a brace, or sometimes surgery, depending on how severe the fracture is. Recovery typically includes physical therapy to help regain strength and mobility in the knee.
